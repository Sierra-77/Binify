FAQ

How should composite materials be classified?

Composite materials, like food packaging bags and paper-plastic composites, are difficult to recycle. Check for recycling symbols on the packaging. Non-recyclable items should be disposed of as other waste.

Does improper sorting affect recycling?

Yes. Incorrect sorting can contaminate recyclables and make them unusable. For example, mixing plastic with food waste can prevent recycling.

MDF

Misconception: All plastics are recyclable.

Plastic containers with oil stains or food residue should be cleaned before recycling, or disposed of as other waste.

Misconception: Wet wipes can go in the recycling bin.

Wet wipes often contain chemicals and materials that aren't recyclable, so they should be disposed of as other waste.
